About

Built by an operator. Measured in results.

Trinacria Advisors is an operator led advisory firm. It is built and run by someone who has started companies, run them, grown them, and sold them, and who now uses modern AI and software as the edge that makes that work faster and the results measurable. This is not an AI consultancy learning how businesses run. It is a business operator who mastered the technology. The mandate can be a system, a function, or the whole company.

The name

Trinacria is the ancient emblem of Sicily.

Three legs, always in motion, around a head that thinks. That is the firm: constant forward motion, grounded in judgment.

How the firm works

Founder led, always.

Every engagement is led by the founder. Judgment does not delegate.

Staffed to the mandate.

Engagements are scoped so senior consultants and engineers can join under the founder's direction as the work requires. The bench grows with the mandate; the standard does not move.

Multiplied by the build system.

The firm's AI build discipline lets a senior team deliver what used to take a department, in weeks instead of quarters.

The founder

Joe Divona

Joe Divona has spent more than three decades in audio visual, IT, and low voltage integration, including fourteen years running a national design and installation company with global accounts and full P&L ownership. That includes years as an IT executive responsible for the infrastructure real businesses run on. He has lived the complete company lifecycle from startup through acquisition. He holds active California C10 and C7 contractor licenses, so he speaks the language of the field as fluently as the language of the boardroom.

He has been building the technology backbone of businesses since long before AI made it fashionable. He designed the custom Salesforce CRM that ran his own company and earned a 2007 Microsoft implementation award for an ERP deployment. The pattern has never changed: adopt the strongest tools early, wire them into how the business actually operates, and measure the result.

Today he leads Trinacria Advisors across advisory, operating, and build mandates. Recent work spans company wide AI deployment at a specialty contractor, executive financial reporting, revenue recovery, and workforce development, with production systems in daily client use across finance, operations, and executive reporting.

Operating philosophy

Operator first, technologist second.

Most people arriving at AI and technology consulting came from software and are learning the industries. This firm came up the other way, through the trades, the field, and the P&L. When systems get built here, they are grounded in how the work actually runs, not bolted on from the outside.

Translate between the field and the boardroom.

Leadership, sales, engineering, project management, purchasing, and field crews rarely speak the same language, and the gaps between them are where margin disappears. This firm turns technical reality into executive action and executive priorities into plans a crew can run.

Production over pilots.

An experiment that never leaves the lab is a cost. A roadmap nobody executes is a document. The standard here is a working system, in production, in daily use.

The hard part is never the model.

The lesson from deploying nearly a dozen production systems: the model is the easy part. The hard parts are integration, data quality, and earning enough trust that people use the tool every day. Build for those and the system sticks. Skip them and you get another abandoned pilot.

Process before software.

Software does not fix unclear ownership, inconsistent handoffs, or weak documentation. The process comes first: who owns what, when decisions happen, how information moves, and what gets measured. Then the software makes the process fast.

Make the work visible.

Hidden work becomes missed deadlines, surprise costs, and burned out teams. Dashboards, trackers, and reporting exist so leadership sees the problem before the customer does.

Highest value problem first.

Engagements do not start with a technology. They start with where the most money, time, or risk is sitting, and they attack that first.

The client owns everything.

Code, data, documentation, and the knowledge to run it. No dependency by design.

Weeks, not quarters.

Working systems arrive fast, get proven in real use, and improve from there.

Direct by default.

Plain answers, honest tradeoffs, and deliverables an executive can act on the day they land.
